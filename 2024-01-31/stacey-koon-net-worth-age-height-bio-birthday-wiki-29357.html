    Stacey Koon Biography

    Stacey Koon is one of the most popular and richest Police Officer who was born on November 23, 1950 in Lynwood, United States. Stacey Cornell Koon (born November 23rd 1950) was an ex-sergeant of the Los Angeles Police Department who became famous internationally following Rodney King. Rodney King incident.

    In 1993, four police officers were confronted in Federal court located at Los Angeles; Koon and Powell were found guilty for violating the civil rights of King. In the United States Federal Sentencing Guidelines suggest that offenders spend as long as ten year in jail. In the end, U.S. District Judge John Davies sentenced the offenders to 30 months. The sentence was later vacated from the United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit in August 1994 because of the violation of guidelines. On February 26, 1996 the decision was annulled by the Supreme Court of the United States in a decision that found the sentence shortened was within the discretion of the district court.

    Koon eventually moved to Castaic, north of Los Angeles. In 2012, he began working as a chauffeur in Los Angeles for the limousine company Music Express where his patrons have included former U.S. vice president Al Gore and actor and political commentator D. L. Hughley.

    In 2018, Koon was arrested for driving under the influence in Santa Clarita, California.

    Both Koon as well as Koon’s fellow LAPD officers Laurence Powell have been used as racist symbols in hip-hop and other related music. Koon is mentioned by the Rap metal group Rage Against the Machine in their hit song “Vietnow”, and is featured in the Ice Cube songs “Really Doe” and “We Had to Tear This Motherfucka Up.” Koon was also named on The Simpsons episode “Sideshow Bob Roberts” by conservative commentator Birch Barlow as an example of someone who was “railroaded by our liberal justice system.” Koon was twice parodied during 1993, by Jim Carrey on the American sketch comedy TV show In Living Color.
