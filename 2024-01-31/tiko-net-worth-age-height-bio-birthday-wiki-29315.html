    In Pamplona, Navarre, Tiko was a star player for his the club he grew up with, CA Osasuna, alternating between the A and B-sides of the club for several years while the latter played within the 2nd division. His debut as a professional came on 10 May 1997 when Tiko scored an injury-time winner against CD Badajoz (1-0).

    In 1999 and 2000, Tiko signed with neighbours Athletic Bilbao, making his La Liga debut on 12 October 1999, in the 4-3 win away against Malaga CF. He played 20 times in every competition during the season, scoring only once.

    In August 2008, deemed surplus to requirements, Tiko joined second level side SD Eibar on a one-year loan deal. He was immediately released upon his return to Athletic following the former’s relegation, and retired from the game shortly after at the age of 33.
