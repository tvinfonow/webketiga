    Napheesa Collier Biography

    Napheesa Collier is one of the most popular and richest Basketball Player who was born on September 23, 1996 in Jefferson City, Missouri, United States. Collier is the grandchild of Gershon Collier who was a lawyer, an ambassador to United Nations, an ambassador to the United States and briefly the chief justice of Sierra Leone. He died two years later, having been an expatriate and educator. Gamal Collier, her father, told The New York Times that he raised Napheesa to understand the importance of self-sufficiency, responsibility, and maintaining the family name. Gershon was instrumental in Sierra Leone’s independence from Great Britain in 1961.

    The Minnesota Lynx selected Collier as the 6th overall selection in the 2019 WNBA Draft. Collier was initially asked to play small forward, power forward, and then small forward. Collier’s debut WNBA game against the Chicago Sky saw her score 27 points, which was the second highest debut for a rookie after Candace Parker. Collier averaged 33.3 minutes per game, which is more than any other WNBA player.

    After Tamika Catchings, Collier is the second rookie in WNBA history with 400 points, 200 rebounds, and 60 steals. Collier is fourth (after Maya Moore and Catchings) player to record a season of 400 points, 200 rebound, 75 assists and 50 steals. Collier was elected to the 2019 WNBA All-Rookie Team and became A’ja Wilson’s injury replacement.
