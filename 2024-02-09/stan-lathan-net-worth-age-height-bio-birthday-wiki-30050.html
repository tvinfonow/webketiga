    Top Facts about Stan Lathan

    1. Stan Lathan was born on July 8, 1945 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.
    2. He earned a Bachelor of Arts in theatre from Pennsylvania State University in 1967 and a master’s degree from Boston University.
    3. Lathan directed the pilots for many sitcoms, including Martin, Moesha, The Parkers and The Steve Harvey Show.
    4. He executive produced and directed Dave Chappelle’s stand-up comedy specials Killin’ Them Softly, Equanimity, The Bird Revelation, Sticks & Stones and The Closer.
    5. Lathan created the stand-up comedy franchise Def Comedy Jam on HBO which ran for 8 seasons.
    6. He co-created Run’s House, a reality series that lasted for five seasons.
    7. Lathan received two Peabody Awards, six NAACP Image Awards, the 2003 Diversity Award, the 2013 Lifetime Achievement Award, the 2017 Trailblazer Award from the Apollo Theater, and two Grammy Awards.
    8. He directed the feature film Beat Street in 1984 for Orion Pictures.
    9. Lathan directed the presidential event An Evening of Poetry, Music and Spoken Word, which was hosted by President Barack Obama and first lady Michelle Obama.
    10. He directed Netflix stand-up comedy special Mo Amer: The Vagabond and HBO stand-up comedy special Amanda Seales: I Be Knowin’ in 2018.
    11. He executive produced and directed Dave Chappelle: Equanimity (2017) which received Primetime Emmy for Outstanding Variety Special (Pre-Recorded).
    12. Lathan won another Grammy Award for the 2019 Best Comedy Album for Dave Chappelle: Equanimity and Dave Chappelle: The Bird Revelation.
    13. In 1973, Lathan began directing network television when he was brought to Los Angeles to direct multiple episodes of Sanford & Son.
    14. He directed dramas for public television’s Great Performances, American Playhouse and The American Short Story.
    15. He was one of the first directors of Sesame Street.
