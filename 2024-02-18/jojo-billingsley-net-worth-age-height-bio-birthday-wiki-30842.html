    JoJo Billingsley Biography

    JoJo Billingsley is one of the most popular and richest Singer who was born on May 28, 1952 in Memphis, United States. Deborah Jo Billingsley (May 28 1952 – June 24 2010,) was an American singer who was a soloist, songwriter, and record producer. Her most well-known work was her collaboration as a member of The Honkettes, backing vocalists for the Southern rock group Lynyrd Skynyrd.

    As of 2005, the singer appeared numerous times as “The Honkettes” in an alternative version of Skynyrd named “The Saturday Night Special Band” which featured the former Skynyrd band members Ed King, Artimus Pyle and Leslie Hawkins, which helped raise funds in aid of the Hurricane Katrina victims.

    Billingsley died in Cullman on June 24, 2010 after a battle with cancer at the age of 58.

    A tragic October 20 plane crash in 1977 resulted in the death of some people from the group as well as road crew members, however Billingsley was the one member of the band not aboard the plane. She said that she had a vision two days prior to the time the plane crashed. She had foreseen the incident. She tried to warn other members of the band to not board the plane. According to Billingsley’s account, this incident was the catalyst for her becoming a new Christian.
