    Lloyd Biography

    Lloyd is one of the most popular and richest Singer who was born on January 3, 1986 in New Orleans, Louisiana, United States. Lloyd Polite Jr. was born January 3, 1986 in New Orleans, Louisiana to Robin Lewis Polite and Lloyd Polite Sr. Lloyd was raised in New Orleans’ Calliope Projects. After high school, he moved to Decatur in Georgia. He discovered his passion for singing in Decatur and moved to New York City with Irv Gotti.

    The singer returned to the studios to work with Bryan-Michael Cox and James Lackey. Jazze Pha and Big Reese were also producers. Jasper Cameron was part of The Inc.’s 7 Aurelius. Street Love was released March 13, 2007. It debuted at number 2 on the US Billboard 200, Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums charts. With sales of 145,000 copies, it became Lloyd’s biggest debut and largest first week. The album was eventually certified gold and has been sold more than half a million times in the United States. Jasper Cameron’s “You”, featuring rapper Lil Wayne was a huge commercial success and became his first Hot 100 top ten single. It also reached the top spot on the Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs charts. It reached number 45 in the United Kingdom, and number 25 in New Zealand. The second single, “Get It Shawty”, reached number 16 on Billboard’s Hot 100 and number 4 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs Charts. It reached number 37 in the United Kingdom. “Player’s Prayer”, the third and final single, peaked at number 7 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs charts. The song was not promoted, and it didn’t get a music video. He was later featured on Huey’s “When I Hustle” and Dem Franchize Boyz’s “Turn Heads” in 2007. Both songs had minor success in the United States and reached number 80 and 75 respectively on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs charts.

    In June 2009, it was announced that Lloyd would depart from The Inc. Records. Lloyd stated that he was “focusing on just growing in general”. A song titled “Pusha”, produced by The Runners written by Lloyd, Raymond “Ray-Ray” Gordon, Sean “Slim” McMillion, and The Monarch and featuring Juelz Santana was leaked online. Juelz Santana was later replaced on the song by Lil Wayne but later both verses were put together and was released as the lead single from his EP, Like Me: The Young Goldie EP. Lloyd also collaborated with Lil Wayne on the song “BedRock” which is the second single from Lil Wayne’s record label Young Money compilation album. On March 13 it was confirmed that Lloyd signed to Interscope Records.

    On September 3, 2010, Lloyd cut off his signature long hair and donated it to charity. On July 6, 2011, Lloyd revealed a new tattoo of “Guns & Roses” that was tattooed on the back of his head. On March 14, 2016 it was announced and revealed that Lloyd had earned his GED certificate. In an August 2016 interview with Sway Calloway, Lloyd explains his hiatus through the lyrics of his song “Tru”, expressing how he lost an unborn child to an abortion and that it “left a big hole”, while also detailing other family issues. In September 2017, Lloyd’s wishes of having a child came true when his girlfriend gave birth to their son River. The two welcomed their daughter in late 2018.

    In 2005, Lloyd acted on television as A-Train (Boy to Main Cast Member Spirit), on UPN’s One on One. He debuted his film acting skills in 2019 as Gregory Williams (founder member of Switch) in TV One’s original biopic The Bobby Debarge story.
