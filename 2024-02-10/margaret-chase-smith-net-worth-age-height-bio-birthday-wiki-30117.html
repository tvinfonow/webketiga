    Top Facts about Margaret Chase Smith

  • Margaret Chase Smith was the first woman to serve in both houses of Congress.
  • She served as a U.S. Senator from Maine for 24 years.
  • Smith was known for her independent and principled stance on issues.
  • In 1950, she delivered her famous “Declaration of Conscience” speech denouncing McCarthyism.
  • Smith ran for president in 1964, becoming the first woman to seek the Republican nomination.
  • She co-sponsored the Equal Rights Amendment and supported women’s rights.
  • Smith was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1989.

  • The Margaret Chase Smith Library is located in her hometown of Skowhegan.
