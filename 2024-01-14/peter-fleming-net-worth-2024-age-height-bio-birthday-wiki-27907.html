    Peter Fleming Biography

    Peter Fleming is one of the most popular and richest Tennis Player who was born on January 21, 1955 in Chatham Borough, United States. Peter Blair Fleming (born January 21st, 1955 in Chatham Borough, New Jersey) is an ex-professional tennis pro in the United States. In his doubles team together with John McEnroe, he won 52 titles, seven of which were the Grand Slams (four at Wimbledon and three of them at the US Open). As a player in singles, He reached World No. 8, and won 3 titles (including in 1979 the Cincinnati Open).

    He was admitted into the Intercollegiate Tennis Association (ITA) Hall of Fame. On July 30, 2011, Fleming, the Farmers Classic 1979 singles champion and doubles champion of 1983 was named the 2011 Farmers Classic Tournament Honoree.

    In in the 80s Fleming was paired together with the fellow American John McEnroe to dominate the men’s doubles match. They have won 50 doubles titles and won four of them at Wimbledon (1979 1981 1983, 1979 and 1984) and three titles at the US Open (1979, 1981 and 1983). Fleming once stated”Andrew Fleming and John McEnroe are “The best doubles pair in the world is John McEnroe and anyone”.
