    Top Facts about Shirley Jackson

  • Shirley Jackson was born in San Francisco in 1916.
  • She is best known for her short story “The Lottery.”
  • Jackson wrote six novels and over 200 short stories.
  • Her work often explored the darker side of human nature.
  • Jackson suffered from anxiety and agoraphobia throughout her life.
  • She was married to literary critic Stanley Edgar Hyman.
  • Jackson died at the age of 48 from heart failure.
  • Her novel “The Haunting of Hill House” has been adapted into multiple TV shows and movies.
  • Jackson’s writing influenced authors such as Stephen King and Neil Gaiman.
  • In 2018, a biopic about her life titled “Shirley” was released starring Elisabeth Moss as Jackson.
