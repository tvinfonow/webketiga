  • Robert Waltrip Short was an American cabaret singer and pianist.

  • He was a regular patron at Ted Hook’s Backstage in New York City.
  • His friend Tom Jobim was present during his final days in New York City.
  • Short wrote two memoirs in 1971 and 1995.
  • He was offered a two-week stint at the Café Carlyle in New York in 1968.
  • Short was inducted as a Laureate of The Lincoln Academy of Illinois in 1983.
  • He was awarded the Order of Lincoln by the governor of Illinois in the area of performing arts.
  • He sang for films and television in the 1970s and 1980s.
  • Short was adopted by Ronald Bell, son of his older brother William.
  • He was acknowledged to be gay, but never publicly declared it.
  • He died from leukemia on March 21, 2005, aged 80.
  • He was buried in Atherton Cemetery in Danville, Illinois.
