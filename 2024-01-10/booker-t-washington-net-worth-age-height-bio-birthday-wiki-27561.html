    Top Facts about Booker T. Washington

  • Born into slavery in Virginia in 1856.
  • Founded Tuskegee Institute in Alabama in 1881.
  • Advocated for vocational education and self-help for African Americans.
  • Delivered the famous “Atlanta Compromise” speech in 1895.
  • Advised several U.S. presidents on race relations and education.
  • Authored several books, including his autobiography “Up From Slavery.”
  • Died on November 14, 1915 at the age of 59.
  • Considered one of the most influential African American leaders of his time.
  • His legacy continues to inspire and impact education today.
