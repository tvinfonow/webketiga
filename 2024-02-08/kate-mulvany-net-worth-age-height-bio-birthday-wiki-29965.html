Mulvany wrote an adaptation of the Ruth Park’s The Harp in the South trilogy into a two-part production to be performed by Sydney Theatre Company. The following year, she followed that by reworking Mary Stuart, the Schiller drama Mary Stuart the first adaptation to be done by a woman to be performed by Sydney Theatre Company. One review stated “Mulvany’s bold adaptation recentres the queens, shearing away nearly every male soliloquy and interaction held exclusively between men, of which there are an abundance in Schiller’s text” Others hailed it as “dazzlingly different”, and it was a “feminist” reimagining of a classic.
