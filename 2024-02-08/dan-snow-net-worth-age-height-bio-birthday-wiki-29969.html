    Dan Snow Biography

    Dan Snow is one of the most popular and richest TV Show Host who was born on December 3, 1978 in Westminster, England, United Kingdom. British television personality , best popular for hosting BBC historical shows such as Battlefield Britain and Montezuma. He was awarded the BAFTA Cymru award for the best Presenter for his role in Harian, the BBC documentary Harian in 2009.

    His father is a media persona also known as the an historian His father is media personality and historian Peter Snow..

    Snow was educated in London at Westfield Primary School now Barnes Primary and at St Paul’s School where he was Captain of School and rowed for its VIII. He then went to Balliol College, Oxford, his father’s alma mater, and graduated with first-class honours in Modern History. A keen rower since his secondary school days, he won the U-23 men’s division at the 2000 British Indoor Rowing Championships and rowed three times in the Boat Race, winning in 2000 and losing the controversial 2001 Boat Race when President.

    Snow presented his first programme in October 2002 just after graduating from university, co-presenting the BBC’s 60th anniversary special on the Battles of El Alamein with his father Peter. Father and son then collaborated to present an eight-part documentary series called Battlefield Britain, which aired in 2004 winning a BAFTA Craft Award for special effects. The same year Snow won a Sony award as one of the presenters on the LBC Boat Race coverage.

    He married criminologist Lady Edwina Grosvenor in November 2010. They have two children together.
