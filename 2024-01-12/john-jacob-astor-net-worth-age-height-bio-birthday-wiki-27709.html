    Top Facts about John Jacob Astor

    1. John Jacob Astor was born in 1763 in Walldorf, Germany and emigrated to the United States in 1784.
    2. He made his fortune through the fur trade, opium smuggling, and investing in real estate, becoming the first multi-millionaire in the US.
    3. Astor founded the American Fur Company on April 6, 1808, and owned numerous subsidiaries with operations in the Great Lakes and Pacific coast regions.
    4. Astor founded the Astor Library for the New York public in his will, which was eventually consolidated into the New York Public Library.
    5. He was president of the German Society of New York from 1837 to 1841, and was a Master Freemason in Holland Lodge #8 in New York City.
    6. Astor was the wealthiest person in the United States at the time of his death in 1848, worth an estimated $20 million (equivalent to $598 million in 2020).
    7. Astor had eight children with his wife Sarah Cox Todd, and left the bulk of his fortune to his second son William.
    8. The Astor Mansion at Hellgate, the pair of marble lions by the entrance of the New York Public Library Main Branch, the neighborhood of Astoria in Queens, the one-block Astor Place Street in Manhattan, and the town of Astoria in Oregon are named after Astor.
    9. Astor founded the Town of Astor in Wisconsin in 1835, which was later incorporated into the Borough of Green Bay.
    10. FC Astoria Walldorf, a German football club, was named after Astor in his and his family’s honor in 1908.
