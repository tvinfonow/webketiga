Geronimo John Allison (born on 18 January 1994) is an American football wide receiver who plays for the Detroit Lions of the National Football League (NFL). He played in the NCAA football tournament at Illinois and was accepted to the Green Bay Packers as an free agent who was not drafted in 2016.

    After being undrafted during the 2015 NFL Draft, Allison signed with the Green Bay Packers on May 6 in 2016.

    Allison was re-signed by the Packers on March 14, 2018. He started the season well, with 19 receptions for 289 yards and two touchdowns before suffering a concussion in Week 4. Allison was placed on injured reserve on November 6, 2018 after undergoing groin surgery.

    On March 13, 2019, Allison was re-signed by the Packers.

    On September 3 in 2016, Allison was dismissed by the Packers during the team’s final cuts, but was re-signed to the Packers the practice team just two days later. On the 24th of October Allison was elevated from the practice team into the roster of active players. Allison began the NFL debut in the game against Atlanta Falcons in Week 8. The first time he made an NFL reception was an eight-yard touchdown by Aaron Rodgers in the second quarter.
