    Kelly Miller is one of the most popular and richest Essayist who was born on July 23, 1863 in South Carolina, South Carolina, United States. Remembered for his numerous racial equality-themed essays and articles, as well as for his book Out of the House of Bondage, this Washington, D.C.-based journalist, author, and prominent African-American intellectual earned the nickname of “Bard of the Potomac.”

    He and W. E. B. Du Bois co-edited the NAACP’s journal The Crisis.
