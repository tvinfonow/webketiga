    Top Facts about Wayne Shorter

  • Wayne Shorter was a legendary American jazz saxophonist and composer.
  • He was born on August 25, 1933 in Newark, New Jersey.
  • Shorter played with Art Blakey’s Jazz Messengers and Miles Davis.
  • He co-founded the jazz fusion band Weather Report in 1970.
  • Shorter won 11 Grammy Awards throughout his career.
  • He composed many iconic jazz standards such as “Footprints” and “Speak No Evil.”
  • In addition to saxophone, he also played the clarinet and flute.
  • Shorter was inducted into the DownBeat Jazz Hall of Fame in 1999.
  • He received an honorary doctorate from Berklee College of Music in 2005.
  • Wayne Shorter passed away on March 22, 2023 at the age of 89.
