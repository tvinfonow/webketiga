    Top Facts about Kit Connor

  • Kit Sebastian Connor was born in the South London Borough of Croydon on 8 March 2004.
  • He starred as Tom Anderson in the holiday comedy Get Santa and as a recurring character Archie Beckles in the CBBC series Rocket’s Island.
  • In 2019, Connor portrayed a teenaged Elton John in the musical film Rocketman and played Joe Woodard in the drama Little Joe.
  • He is set to star in an upcoming film adaptation of the novel A Cuban Girl’s Guide to Tea and Tomorrow by Laura Taylor Namey.
  • In 2021, Connor was cast in the Netflix series Heartstopper, for which he won the inaugural Children’s and Family Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Performance.
  • In October 2022, Connor came out as bisexual in a Twitter post.
  • He had quit Twitter the previous month due to harassment and claims of queerbaiting after he was photographed holding hands with actress Maia Reficco.
  • Connor attended the Hayes Primary School in Kenley and Whitgift School in South Croydon, where he completed his A Levels in drama, English literature and history.
  • He voiced Pantalaimon in the BBC One and HBO fantasy series His Dark Materials.
  • He made his stage debut in Welcome Home, Captain Fox! at Donmar Warehouse.
  • He had roles in the 2018 films The Mercy, The Guernsey Literary and Potato Peel Pie Society, Slaughterhouse Rulez, as well as the BBC One television film Grandpa’s Great Escape.
  • He starred as Alexander in the Old Vic Theatre production of Fanny & Alexander.
  • In March 2023, it was announced that Connor would lead the cast of the upcoming mystery-horror film One Of Us.
  • In his coming out tweet, Connor criticised fans for “forcing an 18 year old to out himself” and having “missed the point of [Heartstopper]”.
  • Connor was supported by Heartstopper creator Alice Oseman, co-stars, journalist Mark Harris, and Labour Party MP Nadia Whittome.
