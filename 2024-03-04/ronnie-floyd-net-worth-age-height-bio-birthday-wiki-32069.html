    Ronnie Floyd Biography

    Ronnie Floyd is one of the most popular and richest Pastor who was born on November 11, 1955 in Gonzales, Texas, United States. Floyd was born 11 November 1955 in Gonzales Texas. In 1978, he received a Bachelor’s of Science degree at Howard Payne University. He then went to Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary in 1980 where he earned a Master in Divinity and a Doctor in Ministry.

    In 1986, Floyd was named Senior Pastor of First Baptist Church of Springdale, Arkansas, USA. Floyd was the leader of his congregation’s construction and opening of The Church at Pinnacle Hills, Rogers, Arkansas, in 2001. He led the adoption of the common name Cross Church by the two churches. Cross Church has expanded to three additional locations, including Fayetteville, Rogers and Neosho. This allows it to reach thousands of people every week. Cross Church is ranked number one in Arkansas, and 18 nationally in total giving to Cooperative Program. This includes nearly 50,000 SBC churches. Cross Church donated $700,000 to SBC’s Cooperative Program in 2013. Floyd also maintains a blog and podcast. He is also a television minister. Floyd is also the founder of Cross Church School of Ministry, which prepares students for ministry and life. Floyd is also the host of the Summit, a weekly lunch seminar that hosts well-known speakers for Northwest Arkansas businesspeople.

    In 2013 he began to lead, along with other pastors, a national Call to Pray initiative. Hundreds of Southern Baptist pastors attended Call to Pray events, all for the purpose of praying for spiritual awakening in our churches and revival in our nation.

    On June 10, 2014, Dr. Floyd was elected president of the Southern Baptist Convention at the SBC’s annual gathering held in Baltimore. Upon close of the meeting, he became the 60th president of the SBC. He succeeded the Rev. Fred Luter. Dr. Floyd served as SBC President until June 15, 2016 when Dr. Steve Gaines was elected.

    Floyd, a strong advocate for evangelism as well as discipleship was a member the “conservative revival” that took control of the Southern Baptist Convention during the 1980s. He was running to be president of the Arkansas Baptist State Convention in 1989, but was defeated at the polls by Mike Huckabee.
