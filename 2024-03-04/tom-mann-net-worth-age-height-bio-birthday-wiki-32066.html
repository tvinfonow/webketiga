    Top Facts about Tom Mann

  • Tom Mann was a British trade unionist and socialist activist.
  • He played a key role in the formation of the Labour Party.
  • Mann was involved in several major strikes, including the 1889 London Dock Strike.
  • He founded the Industrial Syndicalist Education League (ISEL) in 1910.
  • Mann was imprisoned for his involvement in anti-war protests during World War I.
  • He became a member of the Communist Party of Great Britain in 1920.
  • Mann served as General Secretary of the Amalgamated Engineering Union from 1920-1924.
  • He wrote several books on socialism and workers’ rights, including “The General Strike” (1926).
  • Mann emigrated to Australia in 1924 and continued his activism there.
  • He died in Melbourne, Australia, in 1941 at age 85.
