    Top Facts about George Gilder

    1. George Franklin Gilder is an American investor, author, economist, and co-founder of the Discovery Institute.
    2. Gilder attended Hamilton School in New York City, Phillips Exeter Academy, and Harvard University.
    3. Gilder wrote a book in 1981 titled Wealth and Poverty which extended the ideas of his Visible Man (1978) into the realm of economics.
    4. Gilder’s book, Wealth and Poverty, communicated the ideas of supply-side economics to a wide audience in the United States and the world.
    5. Gilder wrote the books Microcosm, about Carver Mead and the CMOS microchip revolution; Telecosm, about the promise of fiber optics; and his latest, The Silicon Eye, about the Foveon X3 sensor.
    6. Gilder is an investor in private companies and serves as the chairman of the advisory board in Israel-based ASOCS.
    7. Gilder was fired as editor of the Ripon Forum in the early 1970s after writing an article in defense of President Richard Nixon’s veto of a day-care bill.
    8. Gilder moved to New Orleans where he wrote Sexual Suicide (1973), which was revised and reissued as Men and Marriage (1986).
    9. Gilder praised mass immigration as an economic boon in both the US and Israel.
    10. Gilder bought the conservative political monthly magazine The American Spectator from its founder, Emmett Tyrrell, in the summer of 2000.
    11. Gilder sold The American Spectator back to Tyrrell in 2002 due to experiencing his own financial problems.
    12. Gilder lectures internationally on economics, technology, education, and social theory.
    13. In Wealth and Poverty, Gilder argued the moral superiority of supply-side capitalism over the alternatives.
    14. In 1994, Gilder wrote that the poor in America are “ruined by the overflow of American prosperity” and “moral decay” and that they are in need Christian teaching from the churches.
    15. In 1991 Gilder cofounded the Discovery Institute with Bruce Chapman, which later became the leading thinktank of the intelligent design movement.
