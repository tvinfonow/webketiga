    Derek Thomas is one of the most popular and richest Politician who was born on July 20, 1972 in Cornwall, United Kingdom. Derek Gordon Thomas (born 20 July 1972) is a British Conservative Party politician and an ex-property developer with an ethnic Cornish origin. He has served as elected the MP (MP) in St Ives since the general election in May 2015.

    Thomas continued to contest in the St Ives seat in the general election in 2017. His campaign also included a visit by Secretary of State of Environment, Food and Rural Affairs Andrea Leadsom to his Penzance office. Thomas was elected again with a higher percentage of votes, but the majority was smaller, at 312.

    In June 2019, Thomas supported the establishment of a Marine Conservation Zone west of Land’s End, calling the move a “big step forward”.

    In May 2019, Cornish MPs received local scrutiny following release of expenses figures for that year, Thomas claimed £184,937, therefore making him Cornwall’s “second most expensive MP” after Steve Double. Thomas said: “Having run my own business before being elected an MP, I am acutely aware of the need to keep a close eye on what is being spent. I constantly look at ways of reducing my expenses but believe my claims are very reasonable for an MP representing a constituency in the extreme south west of the country”.

    In January of 2016 in January of 2016, The Labour Party unsuccessfully proposed an amendment to Parliament which would have required private landlords improve their properties “fit for human habitation”. According to the list of interest, Thomas was one of 72 Conservative MPs who opposed the amendment , who had personally earned income from renting out properties. Thomas was one of the 72 Conservative MPs who voted against it. Conservative Government had responded to the amendment saying that they believed that homes are meant to be suitable to be used by humans, but they were unable to approve the new law that would expressly oblige it. Thomas was a supporter of the Leave campaign during 2016.
