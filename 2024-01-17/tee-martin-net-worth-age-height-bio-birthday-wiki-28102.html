    Tee Martin Biography

    Tee Martin is one of the most popular and richest American Football Player who was born on July 25, 1978 in Mobile, Alabama, United States. Tamaurice Nigel “Tee” Martin (born on July 25 in 1978) is the assistant head coach of the Tennessee Volunteers football team. The offensive coordinator was he as well as broad receivers’ coordinator for the USC Trojans from 2016 to 2018. He was also the quarterback of the Tennessee Volunteers during their 1998 national championships. He was also a player for the National Football League and Canadian Football League. He was among the six quarterbacks picked prior to Tom Brady in the 2000 NFL Draft when he was chosen for Pittsburgh Steelers in the 5th round. Pittsburgh Steelers in the 5th round.

    Martin was selected in the fifth selection with the 163rd overall selection in 2000’s NFL Draft by the Pittsburgh Steelers. In 2004 Martin quit as a player of the Oakland Raiders after four NFL seasons. Martin played one season in the NFL Europe league. In 2002, Martin was instrumental in leading his team the Rhein Fire to an league-leading 7-3 record. The Fire were beaten to the World Bowl, falling 20-26 to the Berlin Thunder.

    In February 2012, Martin was announced as the new wide receivers coach for the University of Southern California under head coach Lane Kiffin. He had been linked with jobs at both Alabama and Oregon previously. News of his hiring at USC was broken by a tweet by quarterback Matt Barkley. Martin replaced Ted Gilmore who left to take a job at the Oakland Raiders. On December 18, 2015, Martin was promoted to offensive coordinator for the Trojans under head coach Clay Helton. On December 27, 2018, after a 5-7 season, Martin became a casualty of a staff shakeup and was fired from the position.

    It was revealed on January 15, 2019 that Martin will be joining Jeremy Pruitt’s staff at Tennessee as wide receivers coach.

    As of 1999, Martin played a key role in leading his team of Vols towards their third straight BCS bowl, which ended in a 31-21 defeat against #3 Nebraska during the Fiesta Bowl. In Martin’s two seasons as an active player at Tennessee in 1999, Tennessee Vols were 11-1 against six of their conference foes (2-0 against.
