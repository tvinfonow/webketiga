Patrick Franklin Kelly (born October 14, 1967) is a former Major League Baseball infielder who played in the major leagues for nine seasons, seven of them with the New York Yankees.

    Kelly batted and threw right-handed. His career Major League totals are .249 batting average, 36 home runs, and 495 hits in 681 games. He moved to Australia in 2001 and scouted for American baseball teams there. In 2009, he became an assistant coach with the Australia national baseball team. At the same time, he became the general manager for the Adelaide Bite in the Australian Baseball League team.

    Kelly played seven seasons with the Yankees (1991–1997), spent 1998 with St. Louis Cardinals, and spent the first part of the 1999 season with the Toronto Blue Jays. He played his final MLB game on June 5, 1999, retiring on March 22, 2000.
