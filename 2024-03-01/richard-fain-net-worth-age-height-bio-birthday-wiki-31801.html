    Richard Fain Biography

    Richard Fain is one of the most popular and richest American Football Player who was born on February 29, 1968 in North Fort Myers, North Fort Myers, United States. Richard Alexander Fain (born February 29, 1968) is an American former professional football player who was a cornerback in the National Football League (NFL) for two seasons during the early 1990s. Fain played college football for the Florida Gators, and thereafter, he played professionally for the Cincinnati Bengals, Phoenix Cardinals and Chicago Bears of the NFL.

    The Cincinnati Bengals selected Fain in the sixth round (157th pick overall) of the 1991 NFL Draft, and he played for the Bengals and Phoenix Cardinals during the 1991 season. He played his second and final year in the NFL for the Chicago Bears in 1992. In Fain’s two-season NFL career, he played in twenty-four regular season games and started eight of them.
