    Brandon Matthews Biography

    Brandon Matthews is one of the most popular and richest Golfer who was born on July 27, 1994 in Dupont, Pennsylvania, United States. Brandon Michael Matthews (born July 27th 1994) is an American professional golfer from Dupont, Pennsylvania. In March of 2017, Matthews won the Molino Canuelas championship on the 2017 PGA Tour Latinoamerica.

    Matthews had a great however “scary” 2017 season, overcoming a absence due to a herniated disc as well as two bulging discs to gain an upgrade to an PGA Tour Latinoamerica to the Web.com Tour. Matthews received the invitation to participate in the Final Stage of Q School for the Web.com Tour after finishing his rookie season in the PGA Tour Latinoamerica. Matthews finished in the top 45, earning conditionsal status for the Web.com Tour. The conditional status guarantees the player a spot in any and every one of the 8 tournaments in the series.

    In November 2019, Matthews lost the Visa Open de Argentina title in a playoff after a fan with Down syndrome made a loud distracting noise. Matthews went on record absolving the fan of responsibility for his loss and gave him a signed glove. The gesture earned Matthews a sponsor exemption into the 2020 Arnold Palmer Invitational on the PGA Tour.

    Matthews became a professional in October of 2016. He was 9th at the Mexican qualifying tournament for his 2017 PGA Tour Latinoamerica, earning his card for the 2017 season. Matthews enjoyed a successful beginning to his professional career being 9th in the standings during his very first event which was in the Avianca Colombia Open. He also ended up taking home his second win in that of the Molino Canuelas Championship.
