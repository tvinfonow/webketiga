    Shaun Micheel Biography

    Shaun Micheel is one of the most popular and richest Golfer who was born on January 5, 1969 in Orlando, United States. Shaun Carl Micheel (born January 5 1969) is an American professional golfer best known for his surprising win at 2003’s PGA Championship.

    Micheel is the second golfer to score an eagle double (albatross) during U.S. Open history. It happened at the 6th hole of one of the rounds in the U.S. Open.

    Micheel is one of the few golfers to have a major as his only PGA Tour win. Micheel has 397 starts through the end of the 2018–19 season, the most of any golfer whose only win was a major. He last played a full season in 2011, competing in the PGA Championship and other events through past champion status.

    Micheel began playing the PGA Tour Champions in 2019.

    At the end of July 2006 Micheel was back in the spotlight after finishing second over Tiger Woods at the PGA Championship at Medinah Country Club; he was able to follow that up with a T7 finish just two days later in the Deutsche Bank Championship. Micheel was also the runner-up in the 2006 HSBC World Match Play Championship after beating Woods in the opening round. In the PGA Tour, he ended the year with nine consecutive cuts , and was at the top of the list of earnings.

    Shaun Micheel is one of the richest Golfer from United States. According to our analysis, Wikipedia, Forbes & Business Insider, Shaun Micheel's net worth $5 Million. (Last Update: December 11, 2023)

    Micheel is a native of Orlando, Florida. He was a student at Christian Brothers High School in Memphis, Tennessee and Indiana University and became a professional in 1992. He was able to teach himself to golf when his family purchased the property on an 18-hole Golf course located in Memphis. He had a rather rocky beginning to his career, and it was difficult to get to his membership to the PGA Tour. His achievements included a win at the Singapore Open in 1998 and an appearance in the Nike Tour (now the Web.com Tour) in 1999.

    He entered the 2003 PGA Championship at Oak Hill Country Club with a rank of 169th in the Official World Golf Ranking and making his 163rd PGA Tour start, becoming one of the top underdogs to win a major tournament in recent times. In the opening 2 rounds, the PGA Champion scored 70-68 (-3) to secure two shots ahead of Billy Andrade and Mike Weir. A round of 69 in the third put him at -4 and tied to the leader together with Chad Campbell and three shots ahead of Weir. He scored 70-par during the last round to beat Campbell in two strokes. In the same year, he was 32nd on the list of money winners. He ranked in his way to the top of the PGA Tour money list for the second time in his career however, he was not able to achieve the status of being a regular high-finisher. His best career ranking on the world stage was 36th in 2004.

    In April 2005, after experiencing months of fatigue, mood changes, and poor play, Micheel began treatment for low testosterone (“Low T”, or hypogonadism). He claimed that his testosterone levels had declined to those of “a man in his mid-70s.” After beginning treatment, his testosterone levels returned to normal, and he reported that his drive and energy had also returned. His condition was widely publicized during the coverage of the 2006 PGA Championship. On April 18, 2014, after having coped with inability to exercise without being short of breath, Micheel underwent heart surgery and had four stents inserted.
