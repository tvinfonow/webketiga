    Jewell Loyd Biography

    Jewell Loyd is one of the most popular and richest Basketball Player who was born on October 5, 1993 in Lincolnwood, Illinois, United States. Jewell Loyd (born on the 5th of October, 1993) is an American professional basketball player who plays for Perfumerias Avenida of Spain’s Liga Femenina de Baloncesto and the Seattle Storm of the Women’s National Basketball Association (WNBA). She was the first player selected during the 2014 WNBA Draft by the Seattle Storm. She played in the NCAA basketball tournament in the NCAA tournament at Notre Dame.

    Loyd was selected first all-time in the 2015 WNBA Draft by the Seattle Storm. She played alongside veteran superstar points guard Sue Bird. Loyd was a role of a point guard before beginning her WNBA career, however due to Bird already playing as a point guard with Storm Storm, Loyd instead played as an offensive guard. Loyd had also been a backup point guard during her first season with the Storm. As Bird was absent for the last 7 games in the regular season in order to recover, Loyd was the starting point guard. Loyd played in 34 games and 23 starts in her debut season with the Storm. Her performance in the season earned her the WNBA Rookie of the Year Award, and she averaged 10.7 per game.

    On July 20, 2018, Loyd scored a season-high 31 points in a 78–65 victory against the Connecticut Sun. In the 2018 season, Loyd was voted into the 2018 WNBA All-Star Game, making it her first career all-star appearance. Loyd finished off the season, averaging 15.5 ppg as the Storm finished 26–8 with the number 1 seed in the league, receiving a double-bye to the semi-finals and homecourt advantage. In the semi-finals, the Storm defeated the Phoenix Mercury in five games, advancing to the WNBA Finals for the first time since 2010. In the Finals, the Storm defeated the Washington Mystics in a three-game sweep, winning their first championship in 8 years.

    On June 21, 2019, Loyd scored a season-high 23 points while tying a career-high 5 three-pointers in an 82–64 victory over the Los Angeles Sparks. Loyd would also make her second all-star appearance in the 2019 season. By the end of the season, the Storm were the number 6 seed with an 18–16 record. The Storm were unable to defend their title in the playoffs as they were eliminated in the second round elimination game by the Sparks.

    Following her rookie WNBA campaign, Loyd played for Galatasaray in Turkey during the offseason 2015-16. She averaged 14.9 points per game, 3.9 rpg and 2.3 APG over 17 contests. In August of 2016, Loyd signed with the Shanxi Flame of the Chinese League for the offseason of 2016-17. The following year, Loyd signed with Guri KDB Life Winnus of the Korean League for the 2017-18 offseason. In August of 2018, Loyd signed with Botas SK of the Turkish League for the 2018-19 season. In the year 2019, Loyd signed with Perfumerias Avenida of the Spanish League for the 2019-20 offseason.
