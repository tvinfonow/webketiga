    Bobby Ray Parks Jr. Biography

    Bobby Ray Parks Jr. is one of the most popular and richest Filipino-Basketball Player who was born on February 19, 1993 in Parañaque, Metro Manila, Philippines. Parks was born in Metro Manila to Bobby Parks Sr. and Marifer Celine Barbosa. His father played basketball throughout the Philippines. Parks Sr. had been selected 58th overall in the final round of 1984’s NBA draft. He became an eight-time Philippine Basketball Association (PBA) Best Import awardee and Hall of Inductee. Parks Sr. and Barbosa were separated. Barbosa relocated from Los Angeles in 2003, while Parks Sr. left for Memphis, Tennessee, in 2005. Parks as well as his younger sister Celine resided living in the Philippines until the year 2006 which was the year his sister moved to live with her mother, and he moved to stay with his father and his stepfamily, also Filipino.

    In September of 2010, Parks enrolled in a computer class located at National University in Manila with the hope to play for NU’s NU Bulldogs in 2011. Because Parks was from an international secondary school, he needed to withdraw from the team before the 2011 season that meant he could not be awarded the Rookie of the Year award since the team would not be considered to be a fresh graduate. It’s no surprise that Parks was still eligible for the MVP award and eventually took home the award during his debut University Athletic Association of the Philippines (UAAP) season with the Bulldogs and the Bulldogs, as Parks was averaging 20.0 scores, 6.5 rebounding, 2.9 assists, 1.1 blocks and 1.1 steals per game. He earned the title of the Most Valuable Player of seasons in 74 and 75.

    Parks made his debut with the Legends on November 23, 2015 against the Austin Spurs. He went scoreless in 10 minutes of play and went 0-of-2 from the field. On December 13, 2015, he scored his first basket for the Legends. Parks’ putback layup with 14.5 seconds remaining gave him his first-ever basket in the D-League. He also finished with one assist and three rebounds in six minutes of action against the Sioux Falls Skyforce. On April 1, 2016, he scored a career-high 16 points against the Oklahoma City Blue. In 2015–16, Parks averaged 4.6 points and 1.9 rebounds in 32 games.

    On October 31, 2015, Parks was selected by the Texas Legends in the second round of the 2015 NBA Development League Draft. He became the second Filipino to be drafted in the NBA D-League after Japeth Aguilar in 2012.
