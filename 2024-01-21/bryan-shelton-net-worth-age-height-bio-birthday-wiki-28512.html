    Bryan Shelton Biography

    Bryan Shelton is one of the most popular and richest Tennis Player who was born on December 22, 1965 in Huntsville, Alabama, United States. Bryan Shelton (born December 22 1965) is a American tennis coach at a college and a Former professional player. Shelton played for the collegiate team at Georgia Tech from 1985 to 1988. He then played professionally from 1989 until 1997. Then, he returned to Georgia Tech, his alma mater to be the coach of his Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets women’s tennis team, who took home the NCAA Women’s Tennis Championship in 2007. The team is now the coach in charge of Florida Gators men’s tennis team from the University of Florida.

    Shelton officially quit the professional circuit in 1997. He then was appointed as a United States Tennis Association (USTA) National Coach, a post he held from January 1998 through June 1999. Shelton worked with MaliVai who was a 1996 Wimbledon finalist.

    On June 8, 2012, the University of Florida announced that Shelton had been hired as the new head coach of the Florida Gators men’s tennis team.

    Shelton has won his two titles in singles (Newport 1991 and 1992) throughout the course of his career. He also made it to in the final of mixed doubles at the 1992 French Open, partnering Lori McNeil. He was ranked his highest at the top of the ATP Tour on March 23 of 1992, and was ranked at number 55 in the world. his highest doubles rank 52 came on February 28, 1994. He was elected to the Huntsville Madison County Athletic Hall of Fame in the year 2006.
