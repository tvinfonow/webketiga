    Top Facts about Denny Crum

  • Denzel Edwin Crum: Biography, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denny_Crum.
  • Denzel Edwin Crum was an American men’s college basketball coach at the University of Louisville from 1971 to 2001.
  • He guided the Cardinals to two NCAA championships (1980, 1986) and six Final Fours, and was honored in the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame since 1994.
  • He played college ball for UCLA under head coach John Wooden and later became Wooden’s assistant, helping the Bruins to win 3 national championships.
  • He was widely credited with pioneering the now-common strategy of scheduling tough non-conference match-ups early in the season.
  • He compiled a 675–295 (.696) record while at Louisville and took the Cardinals to 23 NCAA Tournaments.
  • 13 of his players were first round NBA draft picks, including 6 in the top 10.
  • He was known for Superior in-game coaching and held a rolled-up program in one hand during games, like Wooden.
  • In 1980, his national championship team was known as the “Doctors of Dunk”.
  • He co-hosted a local radio talk show with former University of Kentucky head coach Joe B. Hall from 2004 to 2014.
  • He founded The Denny Crum Scholarship Foundation, Inc.
  • He was awarded Metro Conference Coach of the Year three times (1979, 1980, 1983).
  • He was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 1994.
  • He was an inaugural inductee of Pierce College’s athletic hall of fame in 2010.
  • He received the Legends of Coaching award given by the John R. Wooden Award Committee in 2002.
  • He had a hunting ranch in eastern Idaho.
