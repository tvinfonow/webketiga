    Shelton took on significant roles in three feature films in 2001, which despite varying degrees of success, helped her establish as a prominent up-and-coming actress. She obtained her first leading part in the teen crime dark comedy Sugar & Spice, directed by Francine McDougall and co-starring Marla Sokoloff, Melissa George and Mena Suvari. She played Diane Weston, the head of a group of high school cheerleaders who conspire and commit armed robbery. The film garnered negative reviews and bombed commercially in theaters, but became somewhat of a cult favorite afterwards. Despite critical reviews, Variety magazine found Shelton to be “good” as “the ridiculously peppy but still smart Diane”, and Brian Orndorf wrote for Film Fodder: “The star who emerges from Spice unscathed is Marley Shelton.
