    Top Facts about Matthew Fisher

  • Matthew Charles Fisher is an English musician, songwriter and record producer best known for his association with Procol Harum.
  • He started playing in bands in his teens, playing bass guitar, and later on, the Hammond M102 organ.
  • In 1967, he joined Procol Harum and played the Hammond organ on their single “A Whiter Shade of Pale”.
  • Despite playing a key role in the song’s creation he was not given the credit he wanted.
  • He rejoined the band in 1991 and quit for a second time in 2004.
  • He is credited with a 40% share of “A Whiter Shade of Pale” after winning a court case in 2006.
  • He obtained a degree in computer programming from Cambridge University in 1995 and became a full-time computer programmer afterwards.
  • He played keyboards for Screaming Lord Sutch’s 1972 album, “Hands of Jack the Ripper” and for David Bowie’s tour in 1972.
  • He has produced and been credited on albums which have gone platinum and gold.
  • In addition to his work with Procol Harum, he produced albums for Robin Trower, James Dewar and Tir Na Nog.
  • He also produced an album by the group Prairie Madness and a 12” single called “All Washed Up” by Northampton Band “Magnolia Siege”.
  • He released two solo albums in 1973 and in 1974, and two more in 1980 and 1981 respectively.
  • He also co-wrote and performed on the soundtrack of the 1968 avant-garde film, Separation.
  • He wrote the instrumental “Theme From Separation” which featured on his 1973 album, “Journey’s End”.
  • He performed piano on David Lanz’s instrumental version of “A Whiter Shade of Pale” in 1988.
