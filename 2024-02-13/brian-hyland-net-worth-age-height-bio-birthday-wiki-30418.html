    Top Facts about Brian Hyland

    1. Brian Hyland (born November 12, 1943) is an American pop singer and instrumentalist.
    2. He was particularly successful during the early 1960s and continued recording into the 1970s.
    3. His debut single was “Rosemary” released in late 1959.
    4. His biggest and first hit single was “Itsy Bitsy Teenie Weenie Yellow Polka Dot Bikini” released in August 1960.
    5. It reached No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart (No. 8 in the UK) and sold 2 million copies in total.
    6. Hyland’s other major hit was “Sealed With A Kiss” which reached No. 3 in 1962 on both the American and UK Singles Chart.
    7. He appeared as himself on the March 6 episode of the game show To Tell the Truth in 1961.
    8. Hyland wrote the song “Mail Order Gun” to commemorate the assassination of President Kennedy in 1963.
    9. He scored several minor hits from 1963-1969 including “The Joker Went Wild” and “Run, Run, Look and See”.
    10. Hyland’s commercial success became limited, but he continued to record country music and folk rock styles.
    11. Hyland attempted several departures from the norm including the psychedelic single “Get the Message”.
    12. He charted two more top 40 hits in 1970, both cover versions: “Gypsy Woman” and “Lonely Teardrops”.
    13. Hyland recorded a duet of “Itsy Bitsy Teenie Weenie Yellow Polkadot Bikini” with Albert West in 1988.
    14. From 1960 to 1977, Hyland recorded a total of eleven albums for several different record companies.
    15. Universal Music has yet to release a CD compilation that includes all of Hyland’s charted singles.
