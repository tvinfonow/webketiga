Jonquel Orthea Jones (born January 5 1994) is a Bosnian and Bahamian professional basketball player who plays for the Connecticut Sun of the Women’s National Basketball Association (WNBA). Jones was selected as the 6th overall selection at the time of the 2016 WNBA Draft. In May 2019 she has also been granted her citizenship in Bosnia and Herzegovina, allowing her to play for the national team.

    In the year 2018, Jones would have a diminished role on the team despite her achievements during the last season. She played in just sixteen of the games she played but was still productive in The Sun as a starter as well as on the bench. At the conclusion of the season Jones was awarded the WNBA Sixth Woman of the Award for the year. The Sun reached the playoffs as the 4 seed, sporting 21-13 records, earning an exemption to in the 2nd round. But the Sun lost a second time in the second round to Phoenix Mercury in the second round elimination game with an overall number of 96-86.

    In 2019, Jones would redeem her starting Center role for the whole season. She would lead the league in rebounds once again and averaged a career-high in blocks, steals and minutes by the end of the season. Jones was also voted into the 2019 WNBA All-Star Game, making it her second all-star appearance and would also make WNBA Second Team. The Sun were a championship contender in the league, finishing with a 23-11 record and the number 2 seed, receiving a double bye to the semi-finals. In the semi-finals, the Sun swept the Los Angeles Sparks 3-0 to advance to the WNBA Finals, making it the franchise’s first finals appearance since 2005. The WNBA Finals series was a hard-fought battle, but the Sun fell to the Washington Mystics in five games.

    After her sophomore season Jones was named the center of the Sun. Jones enjoyed a breakout season in 2017 with her new role as the starting center. After a 0-4 start, Jones led the Sun to their first victory of the season. She scored 23 points with 21 rebounding in a 97-79 victory against the Chicago Sky, making her the 13th player in the history of the league to post a 20-point and 20, rebound performance. On July 8, 2017 the Sun achieved a milestone as they made history by completing the greatest comeback in the history of their franchise after beating their opponents the Washington Mystics 96-92 after overcoming the deficit by 22 points. increased their record to 10-7. Jones scored a team-leading 22 points, and added 9 rebounds in the victory. Jones was named to be a part of the 2016 WNBA All-Star Game, her first ever All-Star Game appearance. Jones was able to finish the season with an average of a double-double of the areas of rebounds and points. She she also hit career highs in rebounding, scoring, and blocked shots. Jones is quickly becoming one of the young stars of the league. She set a single season record for rebounding, taking 403 rebounds during the 2017 season, surpassing Tina Charles previous record of 398 (record was broken by Sylvia Fowles in 2018). Jones won in 2017 the WNBA Most Excellent Player Award. Her stellar season as well as Jasmine Thomas and Alyssa Thomas each emerging as all-stars, could help the Sun to playoffs for the first ever since 2012. They posted 21-13 records being seeded fourth in the league. They were also given an automatic berth to play in the second round of elimination games. In her debut match in the playoffs, Jones recorded 19 points as well as had 15 rebounds during an 88-83 loss against the top seeded Phoenix Mercury.
