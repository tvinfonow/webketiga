    What is the concept of bright line eating?

    Bright Line Eating is a weight loss program that claims to heal food addiction through strict adherence to a highly restrictive eating plan. Although founded by a psychologist, the beliefs and behaviors promoted in this program display many of the hallmarks of disordered eating.

    What are the 4 Bright Lines in bright line eating?

    The four Bright Lines are: Sugar, Flour, Meals, and Quantities. Bright Line Eating is very structured and takes a liberating stand against moderation. We adopt an addiction model, because for many people, eating even a small amount of an addictive food doesn’t make the craving go away—it makes it worse.

    What can you not eat on bright line diet?

    Calling yourself a “bright line eater” means committing to four strict rules and striving to never break them: no eating sugar (including all sweeteners besides fruit) no eating flour (including alternative flours or processed grains) only eat during mealtimes without anything in between.
