Ryan Dale Minor, born January 5, 1974, is an American former third baseman in professional baseball and a college basketball player. From 1998 to 2001, he played in Major League Baseball for the Baltimore Orioles as well as the Montreal Expos. He is best known for his replacement of Cal Ripken Jr. on September 20, 1998, when Ripken’s streak of consecutive games ended.

    Minor was a member of three organizations from 2002 to 2004, as well as stints with the Newark Bears, Atlantic City Surf and the independent Atlantic League of Professional Baseball. For their inaugural 2005 season, Minor joined the Lancaster Barnstormers. On May 17, 2005, he hit his first ever Barnstormers homerun at Clipper Magazine Stadium. Minor slugged.268 in Lancaster’s outfield with 26 homers, a team record, and 99 RBIs. These numbers were tied for third on the Barnstormers team. His career ended with a lifetime average of.265 and 154 homers, as well as 567 RBI.
