    Jason Kokrak is one of the most popular and richest Golfer who was born on May 22, 1985 in North Bay, Ontario, Canada. Jason Kokrak (born May 22 in 1985) was an American professional golfer, who currently is a player in the PGA Tour.

    Kokrak was honoured for being the very first player to join into PGA Tour in 30 years from his hometown and the surrounding area during an event held in the Trumbull Country Club, located in Warren, Ohio on December 21 2011. Kokrak was first introduced to golf by Trumbull Country Club at the age of nine-year-old, by his father. The occasion was attended by over 200 people. It also included good wishes and congratulations from Jerry McGee and Bob Lewis Both of them were ex- PGA Tour professionals.

    In September 2011, Kokrak won his first Nationwide Tour event, the Albertsons Boise Open in Boise, Idaho. His 72-hole score of 266 (-18) at Hillcrest Country Club included rounds of 68, 68, 63, and 67. Kokrak’s third round 63 included consecutive birdies on holes one through six to open the round – one short of the Nationwide Tour record of seven. He won by two strokes and earned $130,500, which vaulted him from 70th to 13th place on the season’s money list. Kokrak was an alternate and the last player to earn entry into the field.

    Kokrak was featured in a four-page article, “The Making of a Bomber” in the March 5, 2012 issue of Golf World. The article cited Kokrak’s development as a junior golfer, his teaching influences, and insights into his equipment specifications.

    Kokrak has a record of six professional wins, including 4 on the eGolf Professional Tour. In 2010 Kokrak had two wins and was the top prize-winning player in the tournament. In the same year Kokrak made his way to the last round of qualification school which earned him status as a conditional player on the Nationwide Tour. In 2011, he won the Championship at St. James Plantation and the Donald Ross Championship. Kokrak was 2nd on the eGolf tour’s earnings list, despite participating in just three events.
