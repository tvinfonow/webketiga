    Sonia Falcone Biography

    Sonia Falcone is one of the most popular and richest Painter who was born on March 27, 1965 in Santa Cruz de la Sierra, Santa Cruz Department, Bolivia. Sonia Falcone, born March 27 1965, Santa Cruz de la Sierra, Bolivia, is a Bolivian artist who is well-known for her installation and painting work. She currently lives within Mexico and is an legal Mexican citizen.

    In 2013 the installation by Falcone, which was colorful and made with spices, dubbed Campo de Color was part of the Latin American Pavilion at the 2013 Venice Biennale. In 2017, the same installation was featured as part of the Nevada Museum of Art in Reno in Nevada. The group exhibit was called “Unsettled”, which also traveled to the Anchorage Museum in Alaska and the in California’s Palm Springs Art Museum.

    Falcone’s work has appeared in several collectives, mainly in Phoenix City Government Convention Center– Civic Plaza, invited by the National Hispanic Woman Corporation. She has had solo exhibitions at the Museum of Phoenix, Calvin Charles Gallery in Scottsdale, Arizona, and “The Gallery” in Cabo San Lucas, Mexico.

    In 2015, Falcone, together with artist José Laura Yapita, presented an Aymara language sound installation at the 2015 Venice Biennale.

    Falcone displayed works on display in La Paz (Nota Gallery) in July 2010 and Santa Cruz (Lorca Gallery) in September 2010- Bolivia and at PINTA New York City (December 2010), Miami International Art Fair (January 2011) and, starting June 6 to PINTA London (June 2011).
