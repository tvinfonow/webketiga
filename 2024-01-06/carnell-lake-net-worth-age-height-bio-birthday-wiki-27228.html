Carnell Augustino lake (born July 15, 1967), is an ex-professional American football player.     In the Steelers’ 51-0 defeat to the Cleveland Browns, his first professional regular season start and debut was made. Lake made five combined tackles on October 15, 1989. He also made two pass deflections and recovered a fumble. In Week 6, he made his first interception in his career during a 17-7 victory over the Cleveland Browns. Lake intercepted a pass from Browns’ quarterback Bernie Kosar with one hand. He was awarded the AFC Defensive Player for Week 6. In Week 9, Lake recorded his first career sack against Broncos’ quarterback John Elway during the Steelers’ 34-7 defeat at the Denver Broncos. In 1989, he finished his rookie season with 70 combined tackles. He also had six forced fumbles recovered and one interception.

    The Pittsburgh Steelers selected Lake in the second round (34th overall) of the 1989 NFL Draft. Lake was the third safety drafted in 1989. The selection of Lake by the Steelers was immediately labeled a reach by NFL draft analyst Mel Kiper.

    On January 15, 1989, Lake played in the East-West Shrine Game and was part of the West who lost 24–6 to the East. On January 21, 1989, Lake was part of Los Angeles Rams’ head coach John Robinson’s South team that defeated the North 13–12. Lake played safety in both games as multiple teams were interested in moving him to safety as he was considered to be too small to continue to play linebacker professionally.

    With a record of 9-7, the Steelers finished third in the AFC Central and earned a wildcard. Lake played in his first playoff game, as the Steelers beat the Houston Oilers 26 to 23 in the AFC Wildcard Game. They lost 24-23 to the Denver Broncos in Week Two of the AFC Divisional round.
