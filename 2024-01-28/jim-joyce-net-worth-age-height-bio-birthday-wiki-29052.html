Joyce was born in Toledo, Ohio, on October 3 1955. He was raised in Toledo and was a graduate of Central Catholic High School in 1973. Then, he attended Bowling Green State University in Bowling Green, Ohio, where the team played baseball. In 1977 the year he completed his degree at the age of Bowling Green with a bachelor of science degree in education.

    On September 16, 1996 Joyce stopped his fellow umpire John Hirschbeck when Hirschbeck angrily entered the Baltimore Orioles clubhouse to confront Roberto Alomar. Prior to the incident, Alomar had made comments about how Hirschbeck’s behavior changed following the death his son.

    In 2009, Joyce was inducted into the Irish American Baseball Hall of Fame.

    Joyce had been the second base umpire for Dallas Braden’s perfect game on May 9, 2010, less than a month before the Galarraga game. He was also the first base umpire for the no-hitter pitched by Carlos Zambrano on September 14, 2008.
